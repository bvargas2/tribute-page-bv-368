Lauren Faust

The Animator Who Redefined Modern Storytelling

"I wanted to prove that My Little Pony didn’t have to be just about tea parties and dress-up—it could be about adventure, friendship, and real emotions."
— Lauren Faust

A Timeline of Achievements:

Early Life & Education (1974-1997)

  • 1974 – Born on July 25 in Annapolis, Maryland, USA.
  • 1992-1997 – Studies animation at the California Institute of the Arts (CalArts).

Early Career & Breakthrough (1997-2004)

  • 1997 – Joins Turner Feature Animation; works as an animator on Cats Don’t Dance.
  • 1999 – Becomes a storyboard artist on The Iron Giant.
  • 1999-2004 – Works as an animator, writer, and storyboard artist on The Powerpuff Girls (Cartoon Network).
  • 2002 – Promoted to supervising director and writer for The Powerpuff Girls Movie.

Success with Foster’s Home & MLP:FiM (2004-2012)

  • 2004-2009 – Co-develops Foster’s Home for Imaginary Friends with Craig McCracken.
  • 2008 – Pitches a new version of My Little Pony to Hasbro.
  • 2010 – My Little Pony: Friendship is Magic premieres on October 10.
  • 2011 – The show gains a massive adult fanbase (*Bronies*).
  • 2012 – Steps down as the showrunner of MLP:FiM after Season 2.

Expanding Influence in Animation (2012-Present)

  • 2012-2013 – Creates Super Best Friends Forever.
  • 2013 – Works on Wander Over Yonder (Disney).
  • 2016-2018 – Develops and executive-produces DC Super Hero Girls.
  • 2019 – Receives an Emmy Award for DC Super Hero Girls.
  • 2021-Present – Continues to work in animation, mentoring creators.
